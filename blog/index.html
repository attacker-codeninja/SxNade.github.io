Human Error!

Sachin

The ever-expanding field of cybersecurity is full of threats and risks that every business owner must be mindful of. Protection of client information and critical business data is a binding requirement nowadays. Social engineering or in simple terms human error in cybersecurity means accidental actions taken by employees and users that causes a security breach to take place or allows easy spread of malware in the organizations network.

The scope of human error is very broad but can be categorized into two types (Woods,2019).

1) Skill-based errors

  • Small mistakes that occur while users perform familiar tasks which they might be performing on daily basis
  • But the employee fails to do the right thing due to short-term lapse or may be because the employee did not pay attention

2) Decision-based errors

  • Small mistakes that occur while users perform familiar tasks which they might be performing on daily basis
  • But the employee fails to do the right thing due to short-term lapse or may be because the employee did not pay attention
Cybercriminals generally follow a well-constructed approach to execute a successful social engineering attack.

Information Gathering

Identifying a target and gathering as much as possible information about it is the critical in any type of attack. Getting background information about victims is the very basic step in identifying weak points which might be utilized to create “The Trap”.

Information Gathering can be categorized into mainly two categories (Borges,2021).

a) Passive Information Gathering:

This method of information gathering mostly involves collecting openly available intel about a target without actual interaction with the target.

b) Active Information Gathering:

This method of information gathering can be thought of more on the aggressive side and involves direct interaction with target and its services.

The Trap

Next step is to lay the bait. The success of this step is highly dependent on the previous step because we need to convince our target into trusting us and believing in our story in other words, we need to win trust of our target and trick them into taking an action that will trigger our attack.

Possible Trap Scenario: Assume that we know email address of our target and some of their friends from some of the intel gathered during the information gathering session. We can send fake emails to the targets from spoofed source addresses of their friends and ask them to download and play a game we made (which could be a malicious apk file giving us remote access to theirphone) or we could convince them into visiting a malicious site (containing a browser exploit or maybe even a simple phishing link). 

The chances of such attacks becoming successful are very high since we trick the target into thinking that they are receiving it from a trustworthy source which means we reduce formation of doubt in target’s mind.
Impersonation could be of the university itself for example the spoofed address could be something like: accounts@utoronto.ca. Legitimate Services like sendgrid can be misused to send spoofed emails and services like these are not even flagged as spam most email providers since they are legitimate and are widely used by businesses.

Execute the Attack

Once the target takes an action our attack will start, and this could be a browser exploit or phishing attack or anything that allows us to compromise the security of the target and thereby gain access to confidential information. 




Covering Our Tracks

After we have successfully executed the attack and succeeded in our goals, we start clearing up evidence, we start removing any malware traces that might lead it back to us during a forensic investigation(Shakeel,2019). 







What makes these attacks hard to identify?

These attacks might seem legitimate at first but hidden under the hood it could be a well-constructed complex attack just like an IDE once we trigger it there is no going back.

Lack of awareness

Much of human blunder comes about from end-users basically not knowing what the correct course of activity is. For case, clients that aren’t mindful of the chance of phishing are distant more likely to drop for phishing endeavors, and somebody not knowing the dangers of open Wi-Fi systems will rapidly have their accreditations collected. General Awareness Traninig ensures that end-users have the information and abilities they require to keep themselves and the trade secure.

How to prevent human error in your business?

social engineering can only occur where there is opportunity to do so, and therefore it is essential to mitigate the risks which causes for such error to occur. End-users will continue making mistakes if they don’t know what the correct actions is and what the risks are. To prevent this, it is important to approach human mistake from both sides to make your organization's perimeter strong.

Reduce the opportunities

Changing your work practices, routines and technologies to systematically reduce the opportunity for error is the best way to start your mitigation efforts. While the way in which you achieve this will depend on the specific activities and environments of your business, there are some common guidelines to mitigating human error opportunities.

Privileged Access Management: safeguards identities with special access or capabilities beyond regular users. 




















Password management: Password management applications allow users to create and store strong passwords without having to remember them or writing them down on physical notes etc. The use of two-factor authentication should be compulsory across your business to add an extra layer of protection to accounts.

While reducing the opportunities for error is essential we should also educate employees on security basics and best practices which will allow them to make better decisions, and also enable them to keep security on their mind. This helps them seek further guidance when they’re not sure what the consequences of a certain action could be..

Security Awareness Training: Human Error can arise in a variety of different ways, it is necessary that employees are trained to a basic level on security topics that they may encounter in their daily work activities. Using email, internet and social media, as well as spearphishing and malware training are just some of the topics that training must cover.

All steps in preparing a social engineering attack are cyclical rather than linear once we have access, we might again perform information gathering or even setup other traps but this time from the recently compromised machine which we might impersonate to perform other attacks. With effective security awareness training and bootcamps we can educate company employees on avoiding these attacks.
